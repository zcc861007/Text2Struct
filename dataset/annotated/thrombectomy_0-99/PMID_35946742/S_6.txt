The mechanical thrombectomy was associated to a reduction in the risk of death of all cause ( 0.168 versus 0.201 ; relative risk of 0.85 ; p = 0.04 ) , improvement in the number of patients with functional independence after 90 days ( 0.457 versus 0.275 ; relative risk of 1.65 ; p < 0.01 ) , and improvement in the rate of revascularization ( 0.762 versus 0.339 ; relative risk of 2.20 ; p < 0.01 ) .
